For integers $a$ and $b$, the smallest
integer $m$ such that $a\mid m$ and
$b\mid m$ is the lowest common
multiple of $a$ and $b$. We make use
of the identity
$$\gcd(a,b)\text{lcm}(a,b)=ab$$
to calculate the lowest common
multiple of $a$ and $b$.
Therefore if we calculate
$$\text{lcm}(a_1, a_2, \dots,a_n)
=\text{lcm}(a_1, \text{lcm}(a_2,
\text{lcm}(\dots, a_n)\dots))$$
we arrive at our answer.
