While we could just brute force the problem, we analyse the problem to see if we can do better.
$$\begin{matrix}
    a^2+b^2=c^2\\
    a+b+c=N
\end{matrix}$$
then we combine these to the equation
$$\begin{align*}
    a^2+b^2&=(N-a-b)^2\\
    a^2+b^2&=N^2-2(a+b)N+(a+b)^2\\
    0&=N^2-2(a+b)N+2ab\\
    N^2&=2(N-a)(N-b)\\
\end{align*}$$
We may therefore enumerate divisors of $N^2/2$ to find our desired pythagorean triple. We can do better still, but the above
is already more than enough. We save further analysis for a future problem.
